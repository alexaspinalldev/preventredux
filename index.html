PREVENT

What you need to know

What is Prevent?

Prevent is a UK initiative that helps keep individuals and communities safe by supporting those who may be vulnerable to radicalization. Its goal is to recognise early signs of concern, raise awareness, and connect people with the right help and guidance before issues escalate.

Prevent is about empowering communities to support each other and create a safe, understanding environment for everyone.

As part of the broader CONTEST counter-terrorism strategy, Prevent focuses on supporting people in a way that helps them make positive choices and stay connected to their communities. It’s not about surveillance or judgment — Prevent is about working together with community leaders, schools, and support services to raise awareness and offer guidance. By understanding the signs and offering help early, Prevent aims to create a more resilient, inclusive society where everyone feels safe and supported.

Signs Someone Might Be at Risk

  • Withdrawing from friends or family
  • Using angry or hateful language
  • Intense interest in specific ideologies
  • Struggling at school, work, or socially
  • Justifying violence or intolerance
  • Becoming secretive about online activity
  • Wearing symbols linked to certain groups

How You Can Help

  • Show empathy and listen without judgment
  • Talk to a safeguarding contact at school or work
  • Reach out to the local Prevent team for advice
  • Keep communication open and supportive
  • Encourage involvement in positive community activities

Contact Us

If you have immediate concerns about terrorism or someone’s safety, please contact local authorities right away.
